üîë FUNDAMENTAL RULE: In Melakarta ragas, Sa (Shadja) and Pa (Panchama) are FIXED and never change. They are called Achala Swaras (immutable notes). Only the other 5 swaras have variations.

Rule 1: Each swara name (Re, Ga, Ma, Dha, Ni) can appear only ONCE in a raga. You cannot use R1 and R2 together.

Rule 2: The same keyboard key cannot represent two different swaras in the same raga. Notice how:

üéº LEVEL 4: Janya Ragas (Derived Ragas - Thousands!) ‚ñº
Janya ragas are derived from the 72 Melakartas by omitting notes or adding variations. There are thousands of janya ragas!
By Number of Notes:
  • Sampurna: 7 notes in arohana and avarohana
  • Shadava: 6 notes (one note missing)
  • Audava: 5 notes (two notes missing)
By Purity (Swara Origin):
  • Upanga: Uses only notes from the parent Melakarta
  • Bhashanga: Borrows extra notes from other Melakartas
Popular Examples:
  • Mohana (Audava) from Shankarabharanam (29)
  • Hindolam (Audava) from Natabhairavi (20)
  • Durga (Audava) from Shankarabharanam (29)
